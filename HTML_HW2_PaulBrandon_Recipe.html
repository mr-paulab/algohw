How to cook hearty Bison Chili

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. organic, free-range bison chuck roast
  • 2 to 3 tbsp organic, free-range bison tallow or beef tallow
  • 4 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 medium sweet or yellow organic onion, finely chopped
  • Organic, free-range bison stock or beef stock
  • 6 oz. organic tomato paste
  • 29 oz. organic fire roasted diced or crushed tomatoes
  • 8 oz. organic tomato sauce
  • 1/4 cup organic guajillo chili powder
  • 1 dried organic ancho or chipotle chili pod, finely chopped or ground
  • 1/2 tsp. spicy smoked paprika
  • 1-1/2 tsp dried organic Mexican oregano
  • 2 tbsp organic corn masa flour
  • salt
    Optional toppings:
  • fresh organic cilantro, shredded or chopped
  • sour cream
  • avocados or guacamole
  • queso asadero or queso chihuahua
  • additional fresh, chopped organic jalapenos or organic Hatch chiles

Instructions:

  1. Dice bison chuck roast into 1/2â€³ cubes.
  2. Heat bison tallow or beef tallow in a large pot or dutch oven over medium heat. (Bison is lean meat that needs a little additional fat.)
  3. Add cubed bison in batches, browning well on all sides. Remove browned pieces and place in a large bowl until all the meat is well browned.
  4. Brown the onions in the remaining tallow until they start to caramelize. (est. 35 minutes)
  5. Add the garlic and sautee with the onions until the garlic turns light brown and onions are caramelized (est. 4 minutes)
  6. Deglaze the pot with broth to scrape together all the onion and garlic bits
  7. Return browned bison to pot with whatever juices collected in bowl.
  8. Cook the mixture for about 2 minutes, stirring often.
  9. Add tomato paste and continue stirring until the paste starts to smell sweet and the aroma deepens.
  10. Add crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, ancho chili, chili powder, cumin, paprika, mexican oregano and salt, then stir.
  11. Simmer for 30 minutes and taste for seasoning.
  12. Add corn masa flour as a thickener and stir.
  13. Simmer uncovered for 1 hour, stirring often to prevent burning. Add beef stock or water as needed to achieve desired consistency.
  14. Simmer uncovered for an additional hour, adding liquid if necessary.
  15. Allow to rest 15 minutes before serving.
  16. Serve with queso asadero or queso chihuahua, fresh cilanto, sour cream, avocado slices or guacamole and fresh, chopped peppers.

Enjoy!
